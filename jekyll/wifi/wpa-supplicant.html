When starting from nixos, you need a network. The KDE UI does not provide anything (or at least, I did not find anything).

As usual, CLI to the rescue, use `wpapassphrase`. 

wpa_passphrase <your-ssid>

This will ask you your passphrase. And output something along those line: 

network={
  ssid="<your-ssid>"
  #psk="<your-pass-in-clear>"
  psk=<your-encrypted-pass>
}

So: 

wpa_passphrase your-ssid >> /etc/wpa_supplicant.conf

Edit /etc/wpasupplicant.conf and remove your clear pass.

Then restart the network service. 

systemctl restart wpa_supplicant.service

Then, check that you successfully connected to your network. 

ifconfig

That's it.
